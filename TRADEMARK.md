# Trademark Policy

The **code** in this repository is free software under [AGPLv3](LICENSE). The **name and
brand** are not — they are separate from the copyright license.

## What this covers

The name **"Commons"** and any associated logos and wordmarks (the "Marks") are
trademarks of the Commons project maintainer.

AGPLv3 grants you rights to the *code*. It does **not** grant any rights to the Marks
(this is standard — see AGPLv3 §7 and §16). Copyright license and trademark are different
things.

## What you may do

- Use, modify, self-host, and redistribute the code under AGPLv3.
- Truthfully state that your product is "built on Commons" or "based on Commons",
  provided you don't imply endorsement or official status.

## What you may not do without written permission

- Use the Marks (or a confusingly similar name) as the name of your fork, product, or
  service.
- Run a hosted service under the name "Commons" (i.e. present a fork as "the official
  hosted Commons").
- Use the logos or wordmarks in a way that suggests affiliation or endorsement.

## Why this exists

The free self-host code and the paid hosted service coexist by design. Trademark
protection is what lets anyone use the code while keeping the *official hosted service*
and the *brand* clearly identifiable and trustworthy for the communities who rely on
them. Fork the code freely — just under your own name.

Questions about trademark use: open an issue or contact the maintainer.
